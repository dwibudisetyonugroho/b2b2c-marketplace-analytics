# Step 1: Business Understanding
## The B2B2C Marketplace Growth & Retention Framework

### 1.1 Executive Context & Operational Reality
In today’s two-sided marketplace ecosystems, growth teams and product retention teams often operate in silos. Marketing departments focus heavily on **B2B Merchant Acquisition**—spending capital to onboard new sellers and populate the platform with inventory. Meanwhile, Product and Customer Success teams focus on **B2C Consumer Loyalty**—trying to ensure that end-users return to make repeat purchases.

The critical business blind spot occurs when these two worlds do not talk to each other. We might be spending millions acquiring high volumes of low-quality merchants whose inventory fails to generate repeat customer behavior. Conversely, we might have a highly sticky customer base that is being starved of quality inventory because our acquisition channels are inefficient.

For this project, utilizing the **Olist Brazilian E-Commerce Dataset (2017–2018)**, I am bridging this operational gap. I am not just analyzing sales; I am tracing the lifecycle of capital efficiency from the moment a business lead is first touched (B2B) down to the longitudinal retention habits of the consumers buying their goods (B2C).

### 1.2 Problem Statement: The "Leaky Bucket" Paradox
Our organization faces a dual-threat growth paradox that requires immediate diagnostic analytics:

1.  **The B2B Capital Allocation Risk:**
    We are currently allocating marketing budget based on top-of-funnel lead volumes (MQLs) rather than downstream merchant value. If we onboard hundreds of sellers via "Social Media" campaigns who close slowly and generate low Gross Merchandise Value (GMV), we are burning cash on an expensive acquisition treadmill. We lack visibility into which channels actually yield high-value, fast-converting merchants.

2.  **The B2C Retention Crisis (The Leaky Bucket):**
    Even if we acquire great merchants, the business model fails if customers buy once and never return. Preliminary observations suggest that while our customer acquisition machine is powerful, our retention engine may be broken. If Month-1 retention rates are hovering near zero, we are operating a "leaky bucket"—dangerously reliant on continuous, expensive ad spend to maintain GMV growth because our existing customer base is not compounding in value.

### 1.3 Project Objectives & Strategic Goals
My objective is to engineer a data pipeline that answers two high-stakes executive questions:

*   **Objective 1 (B2B Efficiency):** Identify which marketing acquisition channels produce sellers with the highest **Revenue Yield per Lead** and the fastest **Funnel Velocity** (Days to Close).
*   **Objective 2 (B2C Health):** Quantify the true **Longitudinal Cohort Retention** of our customer base. Do customers acquired in January 2017 return in February, March, and April? Or do they churn immediately after their first purchase?

### 1.4 Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)
To translate these abstract business problems into concrete database models, I have defined the following specific metrics that will drive our SQL logic and final dashboard:

#### A. B2B Funnel Metrics (Merchant Acquisition)
| KPI Name | Definition | Business Impact |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Conversion Rate (CR%)** | `(Converted Sellers / Total MQLs) * 100` | Measures the efficiency of a channel at turning leads into active inventory providers. |
| **Avg Days to Close** | `DATEDIFF(day, First Contact, Won Date)` | Measures sales velocity. Faster closes mean quicker inventory availability and lower sales overhead. |
| **Revenue Yield per MQL** | `Total GMV Generated by Channel / Total MQLs` | **The North Star Metric.** This tells us the actual financial return on every marketing dollar spent per channel. |

#### B. B2C Loyalty Metrics (Consumer Retention)
| KPI Name | Definition | Business Impact |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Cohort Size** | Count of unique `customer_unique_id` making their *first* purchase in a given month. | Establishes the baseline volume for retention calculations. |
| **Month-N Retention %** | `% of Cohort returning in Month N` | The primary health indicator. A drop below 1% in Month 1 indicates a critical failure in post-purchase engagement. |
| **Churn Rate** | `1 - Retention Rate` | Helps quantify the exact volume of customers lost to competitors or dissatisfaction. |

### 1.5 Stakeholder Analysis & Value Proposition
Who benefits from this analysis, and what actions will they take?

*   **Chief Marketing Officer (CMO):**
    *   *Current Pain:* Blindly scaling "Social" ad spend because it brings in high lead volume.
    *   *Actionable Insight:* My analysis will likely reveal that "Unknown" or "Organic Search" channels yield higher GMV per lead. The CMO can reallocate budget away from low-yield social campaigns toward high-yield organic strategies.
*   **Head of Product / Customer Success:**
    *   *Current Pain:* Unsure why GMV plateaus despite heavy acquisition spend.
    *   *Actionable Insight:* The cohort matrix will visually prove the "leaky bucket." This justifies investing in loyalty programs, email re-engagement flows, and post-purchase support rather than just buying more ads.
*   **Sales Operations Director:**
    *   *Current Pain:* Inefficient sales cycles draining resources.
    *   *Actionable Insight:* By identifying channels with high "Days to Close," sales leadership can tailor their outreach scripts or prioritize faster-converting lead sources.

### 1.6 Scope & Constraints
*   **Temporal Scope:** The analysis covers transactional data from **January 1, 2017, to August 31, 2018**.
*   **Geospatial Scope:** All data pertains to the **Brazilian market**.
*   **Data Privacy:** All `customer_id` and `seller_id` fields are anonymized hashes. We will rely on `customer_unique_id` for longitudinal tracking, as standard `customer_id` changes per transaction in this dataset.
*   **Technical Constraint:** We will perform all heavy aggregation and window calculations in **SQL Server (T-SQL)** to ensure performance, using Python/Power BI strictly for visualization.
